Timeline

1859:

    Nikola Tesla is born. He was born based on Slavonic rites. His birth records show he was born on July 28th.

1875:

    Tesla Enrolls In College. In 1875, Tesla enrolled to a Polytechnic based on a military scholarship. He was a disciplined student and achieved the highest grades possible while passing various exams.

1881:

    Tesla Begins His Career As Chief Electrical Engineer In 1881, he moved to Budapest in Hungary, to work for a telegraph company. Upon arriving, he noticed that the Budapest Telephone Exchange company was not in operation, so he worked at draftsman in another company.

1881:

    Tesla Submits Some Of His First Patents. In March 1885, he met with an attorney known as Lemuel Serrel, to help in the submission of various patents.

1887:

    Tesla Re-imagines The Electric Motor. In 1887, Tesla came up with an induction motor that was operated by alternating current, a form of power that was becoming common in Europe and the USA. This type of power provided various benefits, especially in long-distance power transmission. In particular, the motor was operated by a polyphase current, which produced a rotating magnetic field to operate the motor.
